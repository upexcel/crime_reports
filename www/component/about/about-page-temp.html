CrimeReports - Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

For Citizens

Where does the data on CrimeReports come from?

The data on CrimeReports is sent on an hourly, daily, or weekly basis from more than 1000 participating agencies to the CrimeReports map. Each agency controls their data flow to CrimeReports, including how often they send data, which incidents are included.

I see that my local agency is participating, but I don’t see any data. What’s happening?

Sometimes, agencies that are sending data have technical issues that cause the data to fall out of date. If you notice that the data in a given region has fallen out of date, please click the “Feedback” button in the bottom-right of the page to let us know.

How can I get my local law enforcement agency to join CrimeReports?

The data on CrimeReports comes from a national coalition of police departments and sheriff's offices which choose to proactively share data with their communities. If you would like to see data for your neighborhood on CrimeReports, the best way to do so is to contact your local police or sheriff department to encourage them to join.

How can I sign up for crime alerts?

To sign up for crime alerts, search for an address or pan the map to the area that you are interested in, and click the “Create an Alert” button. From there, you will be walked through the process of choosing the frequency of your alerts, the name of your alert, and which incident types you would like to be notified about.

Please note that the alert you receive will exactly reflect your current view of the map - including how far in or out you are zoomed, which incident types you are currently viewing, and which area of the map you are looking at.

How do I unsubscribe or change my crime alerts?

To pause or delete your crime alerts, log into your account, go to Settings, then click on the My Alerts tab. From there, you can pause an alert, delete it, change the frequency, or change the name of the alert. If you’d like to change the location of the alert, simply delete your existing alert and create a new one for the appropriate location.

Why am I not receiving my alerts?

If you are not receiving your free email crime alerts, click on the feedback button and let us know, we’ll help you sort it out.

What if I have two homes and want to receive alerts for both of them?

No problem! Simply log into your CrimeReports account, navigate to the second area that you would like to receive alerts for, and click the “Create an Alert” button.

I see my local agency is listed as a Featured Agency. What does that mean?

Some agencies who are participating in CrimeReports have made a greater investment in transparency, giving their citizens additional features, functionality, and insight into crime and public safety trends within their communities. To access the additional features of a CrimeReports Plus agency, click on the “Explore” button under a featured agency in the list view, or double-click one of the gold “Explore” icons on the map.

Within a Featured Agency, citizens can:

  • View crime trends from the “Trends” tab in the list view, including an over time view and breakdowns by time of day and incident type.
  • View the raw data that powers CrimeReports by clicking the “Get This Data on Socrata” button in the footer.
  • See the social media links, twitter feed, and website for the agency.

Who pays for CrimeReports?

CrimeReports’ service is paid for by your local law enforcement agency. If you like the service, tell them “thanks” next time you see them.

Why aren’t there any crime details?

CrimeReports relies on individual law enforcement agencies to provide details on crimes. Each agency is different and may provide more or less crime information than others. At minimum, CrimeReports requires four items of information when mapping crimes: type, location, date, and time. All additional information is voluntarily supplied by individual law enforcement agencies.

Why aren’t there any exact addresses?

CrimeReports is committed to providing the public with the most accurate and up-to-date crime information available. However, for the sake of victim privacy, all crime addresses have been rounded to the block level.

Can I post a crime on the map?

CrimeReports works with law enforcement agencies across North America to publish official crime information online. Due to the integrity of the information and service we provide, we cannot accept user-submitted crimes.

If I were the victim of a crime, could I request that a crime be removed from the map?

Unfortunately, CrimeReports does not have the ability or resources to verify the identity or victim status of individual users. If you would like to remove a crime from the map, contact your local law enforcement agency and request that the crime be removed.

Mapping Errors

CrimeReports has a greater than 99% mapping accuracy rate; however, occasionally a crime incident or address may be mapped in the wrong place, have the wrong information, or not appear at all. There are several reasons why these anomalies might occur:

  • Maps Error— If a crime or address does not appear on the map, the map may be outdated and may not recognize the location, this is especially true when looking at new housing developments or new roads. If your address does not appear on the map, and you would like further assistance please click on the feedback button, provide us with details and we’ll look into it.
  • Reporting Error— If a crime is in the wrong place or there is an error in the information (date, time, type, etc.), the error may be part of the original police report itself. Because CrimeReports receives official crime information directly from law enforcement agencies, if an error exists in the original record, that error will be reflected on the map.
  • Upload Delay— Each law enforcement agency records its crime reports in Records Management Systems (RMS) and Computer Aided Dispatch (CAD) systems. If a crime has recently occurred, it will not be posted to the CrimeReports map until after it has been recorded and processed by those systems and then sent to CrimeReports—approximately 24 hours.
  • Upload Schedule— Most agencies upload data every 24 hours; however, some agencies upload their data less frequently. If a crime does not appear right away, it may appear in the next 24 hours or more, depending on the upload schedule of the reporting agency.
  • Victim Privacy— Many agencies choose not to display sexual assaults, domestic disputes, rape, murder, or other serious, violent crimes for the sake of victim privacy.
  • Ongoing Investigations— Some crimes are not displayed because they are part of an ongoing investigation. Prematurely releasing information to the public may harm those investigations.
  • Sex Offender Data— CrimeReports receives a national feed of sex offender data that is updated on a weekly basis. If after a few weeks the sex offender data is still incorrect, click on the feedback button and let us know. We’ll verify the information and make any necessary changes.
  • Expired Data— CrimeReports provides crime information to the public for the previous 6 months for non-featured agencies. Crime data outside the 6 month window will not be shown. For featured agencies crime information to the public can be up to the previous 2 years. Crime data outside the 2 year window will not be shown.
  • Status/Type Error— From time to time, the investigation of a crime leads to the re-classification of the crime type or a complete dismissal of the crime itself. Any change in the status or type of a crime will be reflected on the CrimeReports map.
For Law Enforcement

How do I login to manage my crime data?

If you are a CrimeReports Free customer here’s what you do:
If you historically accessed your Analytics, Predictive or CrimeReports administrative panel using crimereports.com,please update your bookmarks to legacy.crimereports.com to continue to access the admin panel for managing your data. We recommend that you start using commandcentral.com rather than crimereports.com as all admin access will transition to this new URL in the future.



If you are a CrimeReports Plus customer here’s what you do:
If you have not already created an agency account on the new service please email support@socrata.com and the team will set you up with an account. Once your account is setup, login and you’ll see the following window, select Data Management and you will directed to the login page for data administration.

Does CrimeReports reduce crime?

Although no formal, third-party study has been commissioned, there is anecdotal evidence to suggest that public-facing crime mapping—by keeping citizens informed about crime in their area—helps them be more vigilant and implement crime prevention efforts in their homes, workplaces, and communities. In addition, there is anecdotal evidence to suggest that public-facing crime mapping fosters more trust in local law enforcement by members of the community.
